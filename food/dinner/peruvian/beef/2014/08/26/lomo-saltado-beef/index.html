Lomo Saltado 26 August 2014

Steak, onions, and french fries -- the perfect dinner. Peruvian-style lomo saltado is hands-down one of my favorite meals. This meal is best if the meat and onions are left to marinate overnight, but you could let it marinade for as little as an hour if you're in a hurry (seriously though, let it marinade overnight). Chicken can be substituted for the beef if desired, I've made it both ways with success.

Ingredients
Marinade:
  • 1 1/2 tbsp. minced garlic
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 4 tsp. cumin
  • 1 tbsp. pepper
  • 4 tbsp. rice vinegar
  • 4 tbsp. soy sauce
  • 4 tbsp. canola oil
    • Meal:
  • 1 1/2 lbs. tender beef (I used sirloin since it was on sale), sliced
  • cooked rice of your choice
  • 1 medium red onion, cut into thick slices
  • 2-3 potatoes cut into slices, or frozen french fries
  • 1 jalapeno, diced (optional - it makes it pretty spicy!)
  • 15 oz. can diced tomatoes, drained (optional)
    • Recipe
    Marinade -- Prep this the night before

    Stir all of the marinade ingredients together in a medium sized mixing bowl. Once stirred, spoon half of the marinade into a larger bowl.

    Put the sliced onion into the smaller marinade bowl and, using your hands, toss the onion around to coat it in the marinade. Afterwards, put the sliced beef into the larger marinade bowl and toss to coat. Once everything is adequately covered in marinade, put plastic wrap over the bowls and store in the refrigerator overnight.

    It's the night before and dinner is almost halfway done, woo!

    Fries

    If using frozen fries, bake the fries according to the package. For homemade french fries, cut the potatoes into your preferred french fry shape. Cover the bottom of a pan with oil (should be at least 1/4 inch deep) and heat on medium. Test the oil to make sure it is hot enough by dipping an edge of a raw french fry in it; if the oil starts bubbling around the fry, the oil is hot enough. One by one, place the fries into the oil, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the potatoes until golden brown and then remove to drain onto paper towels.

    Meat

    Heat a splash of canola oil in a large skillet on medium-high heat. Once the pan is hot, drop in the marinaded beef. Once one side of the beef slices are seared, stir them around and add in the red onions with the marinade (if including the jalapeno, also add that at this time). After the onions start to turn translucent, add in the tomatoes and stir. Take the skillet off the heat and toss in the french fries, gently toss the fries around the beef mixture to coat in the marinade.

    Plating

    Season rice with a pat of butter, salt, and parsley. Serve beef marinade mixture alongside or on top of rice.
