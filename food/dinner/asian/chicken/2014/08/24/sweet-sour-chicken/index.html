Sweet and Sour Chicken
Welcome to Jekyll!
Yo Jekyll!

I love sweet and sour chicken, but I always feel bad for eating fried chicken drenched in deliousness over rice. Instead of gorging myself at my favorite Chinese restaurant, I decided to skip the guilt and make "healthy" sweet and sour at home -- no breaded chicken and lots of veggies! It's at least slightly healthier... right? :)

If you aren't a heavy sauce person, or are serving less than 5 people, you might want to halve the sauce recipe. With the amount of sauce made below, I was able to get 4 servings out of it and have enough left over for at least another 2-4 servings (and I really like sauce).

Ingredients
Sauce:
  • 1 1/2 c. white sugar (3/4 c. for halved)
  • 1/2 c. ketchup (1/4 c. for halved)
  • 1 c. white vinegar (1/2 c. for halved)
  • 2 tbsp. soy sauce (1 tbsp. for halved)
  • 2 tsp. garlic salt (1 tsp. for halved)
    • (If you're like me and never have garlic salt, you can make you own by mixing salt and garlic powder in a 3:1 ratio. I used 3 tbsp. salt and 1 tbsp. garlic so I'd have leftovers.)
    Meal:
  • 5 chicken breasts
  • cooked rice (I used brown, but white is fine, too)
  • 1 small red onion
  • 1 green pepper
  • handful of cherry tomatoes (optional)
  • handful of pineapple chunks (optional)
    • Recipe
    Chicken

    If your chicken isn't already cooked, heat an oiled skillet on medium heat. Once hot, throw in the chicken and cook until it is 170 degrees, flipping occassionally. (If the chicken is frozen, this will take ~30 minutes, otherwise it should take ~20 minutes). Start the sauce while the chicken is cooking for maximum flavor.

    Sauce

    Mix all of the sauce ingredients together in a saucepan. Heat the pan on medium heat until it comes to a simmer. Once the sauce is simmering, stir it and turn the heat down to medium-low. Allow the sauce to simmer for at least 25-30 minutes (or longer!). If you're in a hurry you could get away with simmering for 10 minutes, but it will be pretty vinegary. Stir occassionally so it doesn't burn to the bottom of the pan.

    Rice

    If your rice isn't already cooked, and you haven't started it, you should really start it now. My rice was already cooked so once the veggies were mostly cooked I pushed them to the side in the skillet and heated the rice in the same skillet to avoid doing more dishes. :)

    Veggies

    Chop the onion and pepper into chunks. Halve the cherry tomatoes and large pieces of pineapple if you're adding them. Once the chicken is done cooking, put it on a cutting board to rest. Put a little more oil in the skillet the chicken was cooked in and throw in your veggies. Cook, stirring occasionally, until they're tender (probably ~5 minutes).

    Plating

    Slice the chicken into bite-size pieces. Put some rice on a plate, top with veggies, then chicken. Once the meal is assembled, smother it in the sweet and sour sauce and enjoy.
